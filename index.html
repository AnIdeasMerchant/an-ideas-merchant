The Problem

When everything feels important, how do you decide what actually matters?

Something isn't quite landing, and you know it.

Maybe the content isn't converting. The offer makes sense in your head but falls flat when you try to explain it. The brand looks fine, but no longer feels like you. Or you're busy, genuinely busy, but somehow still not moving towards the thing you actually want.

Each problem looks different on the surface.

Often, they're all pointing to the same thing: you've started solving before you've fully understood what needed solving.

You've probably been told the answer is more content. Better photos. A new website. Maybe.

But first: what's the actual problem? That's where An Ideas Merchant comes in.

The Process

Target Practice.

A five-stage clarity framework designed from user experience and brand strategy systems.

It's a lens to see what matters, a filter to cut through the noise, and a container to move you from tension, to clarity, and into a desired outcome.

The AIM Five-Stage Framework

Every project starts with a real tension, even if it hasn't been named yet. This stage gets underneath the brief to find what's actually driving the work, and produces a single sentence that becomes the anchor for everything after it.

→Output
The Core Tension Statement

Before a brand can speak, it has to know what it is and what it refuses to be. This stage defines the role it plays in someone's life, guide, rebel, translator or protector, and sets the filter every future decision gets run through.

→Output
The Decision Filter

Demographics don't tell you who someone is. This stage gets at what they're actually tired of, what they secretly want, and what moment in their life they're standing in, so the work speaks to a real person, not a segment.

→Output
The Audience Pressure Point

Every brand is telling a story whether it means to or not. This stage shapes that story deliberately where someone starts, what shifts, who they become on the other side, and turns it into language you can actually use.

→Output
The Narrative Arc

Clarity means nothing without action. This stage cuts the noise down to what actually matters right now, what's premature, and what moves the needle first, so the next 90 days have a spine, not a wish list.
